From jjbross@gmail.com on January 25, 2014 04:34:54

The data available at ftp.mapsforge.org (and download.mapsforge.org) are hosted by Freie Universität Berlin (FUB). This was totally fine when Thilo and I were studying/employed at the FUB. But since I graduated last year there is no one left at the FUB who is actively involved in the mapsforge project. A few days agao, Thilo and I had a private discussion about the future of the ftp.mapsforge.org service: we came to the conclusion that we have to shut it down in the near future. However, we are aware that such a service is essential, so we hope that the community can manage to setup something similar. There will be a separate mail on the mailing list discussing this topic.

From funkybo...@gmail.com on January 28, 2014 06:25:45

If you have some usage statistics, it would be very nice to have them.

I know a few people in the geocaching community. It would be my pleasure to ask within the Danish geocachers' forum for someone to host it, but I know that they would want to see what they are committing to.

1: How big is the archive (GiB)
2: How much download traffic is there (also GiB)
3: Do you need to run any scripts/services besides ordinay HTTP/FTP? (Do you need to run the map compilation scripts on the server, or will you just be uploading finished map files?)

For all the above questions it would be nice to have historical data (where available) and estimates for the near future.

Also, you could consider running a BitTorrent tracker. Some of the map files are indeed large enough to benefit from being run as BitTorrents.
