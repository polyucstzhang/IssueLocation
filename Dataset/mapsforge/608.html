Sea sometimes bleeds through from under land masses #608

Closed
lincomatic opened this Issue May 14, 2015 · 12 comments

Labels

enhancement

Milestone

  0.5.2

Assignee

No one assigned

3 participants

lincomatic commented May 14, 2015

Sometimes, vertical or horizontal strips of sea bleed through when creating a map by merging OSM data with land polygon and underlying sea rectangle. Here is a detail from us-pacific.map downloaded from mapsforge server:

Collaborator
devemux86 commented May 14, 2015

Ok now that I see the screenshot, what land polygons are you using?

I have seen such artifacts with split land polygons (e.g. from OpenStreetMap Data).

Collaborator
devemux86 commented May 14, 2015

BTW the land polygons used at mapsforge server are the split ones from there.

lincomatic commented May 14, 2015

Yes, those are the exact set that I am using: land-polygons-split-4326. Is there a better set to use?

Collaborator
devemux86 commented May 14, 2015

You can try the non-split edition provided from the same source.
But the map build time will rise significantly for large complex lands.

Alternatively you can change in render theme the land rendering, e.g. put larger stroke at split polygons in order to hide the seams.

lincomatic commented May 14, 2015

OK, I will try those ideas, thanks. I was avoiding the unsplit polygons because of the comment in the mapcreator's landextraction.py:

"# self.landfiles = "land-polygons-complete-4326" # there seems to be a bug in that data"

Collaborator
devemux86 commented May 14, 2015

That comment is from Ludwig, so he could answer better for that.

lincomatic commented May 14, 2015

OK your stroke idea works great! For the benefit of anyone else trying to fix this problem, I empirically found 0.6 to work:
<rule e="way" k="natural" v="nosea">
<area fill="#F4F3F2" stroke="#F4F3F2" stroke-width="0.6"/>
</rule>
Next I will try using the unsplit polygons to see how that affects map size & rendering speed.

lincomatic commented May 14, 2015

What is the best way to bring the question about land-polygons-complete to Ludwig's attention? Should I post to the mailing list?

devemux86 commented May 15, 2015

May be we should also add at nosea in default render themes (Osmarender / RenderThemeV4) a stroke directive for better handling the seams at split land polygons.

A counter argument could be that the stroke will expand slightly the land.

BTW I see the use of stroke-width="1.0" at nosea in OpenAndroMaps themes.

applantation commented May 15, 2015
Re this issue with the unsplit polygons, the problem was that some geometries were invalid, reason unclear, maybe lines crossing the dateline or unclosed polygons. This might be resolved in newer versions of the coastline files, it is worth a try. The coastlines do not change that much, but I advise to update to newer versions once in a while just to keep OSM data for beaches etc in line with the coastline. Ludwig
